I Am Not The Bouncer

I am not the bouncer. I am a computer programmer. Despite my size and appearance, I do not work security at this or any other establishment.

#NotAllLargeMen

I Am Also Not a Bouncer at Any Other Club

This confusion persists across venues. To reiterate, I am not the bouncer at any other club either. I do not hold the necessary security accreditation for the role.

I'm Not Allowed To Decide If You Can Come In

It is not my intention to speak in riddles. If I say something like "I Can't Say If You Are Allowed To Come In, Mate", there is a good reason. And that reason is that I am not the bouncer.

Please Don't Ask How I Transitioned From My Career As A Bouncer To A Computer Programmer, Because I Was Not Previously A Bouncer Either

I have never previously worked as a bouncer. So I did not have a career change away from being a bouncer to become a programmer. Nor am I undergoing a training process where, for example, I work a bouncer while I study Computer Science at univerity.

Some Bars Do Not Have Security On The Door So It Is Possible That No-One Here Is The Bouncer Either, But Particularly Not Me

Only bars that open late typically have security on the door. If you've encountered me before 10pm, it's very possible that no-one at this bar is the bouncer. But I draw particular attention to the issue that I am not the bouncer.

The Transoccupational Experience

I am so regularly mistaken for the bouncer that I wondered if something deeper is going on. I have a neologism to explain my situation: transoccupational. I am not the bouncer, but I am regularly mistaken for one. This is the transoccupational experience.
